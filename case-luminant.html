03.A

An introduction

I believe it’s important to always stay active and push myself creatively. One great way to do this, is through side projects. I try to divide these between Side Hustles and Passion Projects, and Luminant definitely fall into the latter.
Thru trial and error, I’ve designed watch prints, built the webshop and created a seamless drop shopping integration.

03.B

The prints

When I came up with the idea of creating and selling watch prints, it was quite tempting to pick a few of the most populaire timepieces.
‍
However – since this is a passion project, I tried to stay close to my own preference and design the prints I would like to buy myself. Surely this niche would attract like minded shoppers.

03.C

A showcase

I don’t just want to sell a print, I provide something people might want to aspire. The website should reflect this.
‍
When designing the website, I wanted to make sure that it would be clear to people what they where buying, but without being a typical webshop.

03.D

Branding

The prints are the stars of the show, so I wanted to let them shine and keep branding to a minimum. However, the brand does reflect a certain quality standard, so the logo should be able to act stamp of approval.
‍
Since the designs of the prints are very sleek and minimal, I wanted to add some contrast in the logo, by creating something that’s dynamic and has a handwritten feel, to reflect the craftsmanship that goes into creating the final product.
‍
The colours and pattern follow this same line, they add boldness without stealing the spotlight.

03.E

Interactions

Since Luminant is a passion project, I wanted to experiment with interesting animations to push myself in terms of technical feasibility. In the end, deciding to use animations or not, is usually a trade of between gain and effort.
